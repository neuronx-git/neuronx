# Trae Review: Phase A Product Canon

**Date**: 2026-01-29
**Reviewer**: Trae (Independent Auditor)
**Verdict**: REQUEST_CHANGES

## 1. Executive Summary
The Product Canon is directionally strong and internally coherent on the high-level three-layer thesis (Adapters → Deterministic Playbooks → Intelligence), but it is not yet safe to execute against as “single source of truth” because multiple canonical documents disagree on timeline/year mapping, Multi-CRM sequencing, and marketplace/community contribution scope. These inconsistencies are execution-blocking: they will cause mis-scoped plans, mis-set expectations (SOC 2 / enterprise readiness), and contradictory product decisions.

Reviewed files (complete set under `PRODUCT/`): `README.md`, `VISION_CANON.md`, `ICP_AND_PERSONAS.md`, `PRD.md`, `SYSTEM_ARCHITECTURE.md`, `MARKET_STRATEGY.md`, `ROADMAP.md`.

## 2. Risk Assessment
| Risk | Severity | Mitigation Proposed | Trae Comment |
|------|----------|---------------------|--------------|
| Canon timeline contradictions (Year 1/2/3 vs calendar years) | Critical | Choose one authoritative timeline and propagate | Current canon conflicts will produce divergent plans and “wrong by definition” work. |
| Multi-CRM sequencing conflicts (2027 vs 2028+ vs 2029) | Critical | Define explicit Multi-CRM gate + target year and align all docs | Multi-CRM drives architecture and GTM; ambiguity is not tolerable at canon level. |
| Marketplace scope conflicts (curated vs user-submitted vs non-goal) | Major | Define “curated templates” vs “user-submitted marketplace” and per-year scope | Current statements imply mutually exclusive approaches and risk building the wrong system. |
| Audit log immutability vs retention/deletion semantics unclear | Major | Define immutability boundary (append-only) + retention + deletion policy | “Immutable” and “cannot be deleted” conflicts with 90-day retention defaults without clarification. |
| Market sizing and projections lack citations/assumption registry | Major | Add sources + assumption table; mark as hypotheses to validate | Uncited numbers weaken strategy credibility and increase founder risk. |
