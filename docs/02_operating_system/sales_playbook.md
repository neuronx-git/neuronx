## 1. Lead Response Playbook

### 1.1 Speed Principle

The first firm to make meaningful contact wins. Immigration prospects are anxious,
comparison-shopping, and time-sensitive. Meaningful contact = live conversation or
personalized, relevant message -- not a generic autoresponder.

### 1.2 Response Time Standards

| Channel | Target | Method | Fallback |
|---|---|---|---|
| Website form | 5 min | AI call | SMS within 2 min |
| Facebook/Instagram ad | 5 min | AI call | SMS within 2 min |
| Missed phone call | 10 min | Return call | SMS with callback offer |
| Email | 30 min | Personalized email + SMS | None -- 30 min is ceiling |
| WhatsApp | 15 min | WhatsApp reply | SMS |
| Walk-in / referral | Immediate | In-person | Take info, call within 1h |

### 1.3 First Contact Call

**Opening**: "Hi [Name], this is [Agent] from [Firm]. I'm calling because you
just reached out about your immigration inquiry. Is now a good time for a few minutes?"

Rules: Use their name. Reference the firm. Acknowledge THEY reached out.
Ask permission. Warm but professional.

**If not a good time**: Schedule callback. Log. Move on.
**If voicemail**: Leave message (< 30 seconds). Specific. State when you'll try again.

### 1.4 Contact Sequence

| Attempt | Timing | Method |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Within 5 min | AI call + voicemail |
| 2 | 30 min later | SMS |
| 3 | 2 hours later | Call |
| 4 | Next business day | Call + voicemail |
| 5 | 2 business days | SMS |
| 6 | 5 business days | Email |
| 7 | 10 business days | SMS (final) |

After 7 with no response: long-term nurture. Do NOT delete.

### 1.5 Channel Rules

**SMS**: < 160 chars when possible. Prospect's name. ONE clear action. Max 1/day.
**Email**: Subject references their inquiry. Body < 150 words. Booking link.
**Phone**: Never before 9 AM or after 8 PM prospect time. Honor callback times exactly.

## 2. Readiness Assessment Playbook

### 2.1 Purpose

Determine if a prospect should be offered a consultation. Protects consultant time.
Does NOT assess eligibility. Assesses fit.

### 2.2 Dimensions

| # | Dimension | Question | Who |
|---|---|---|---|
| R1 | Program Interest | "Can you tell me about what you're looking to do? PR, work permit, study, sponsorship?" | AI or human |
| R2 | Location | "Are you currently in Canada or outside Canada?" | AI or human |
| R3 | Urgency | "Is there a timeline? A permit expiring or a longer-term plan?" | AI or human |
| R4 | History | "Have you applied with Immigration Canada before? How did it go?" | AI or human |
| R5 | Budget | "Our consultations are [fee]. Professional fees vary by case. Is that something you've budgeted for?" | Human preferred |
| R6 | Complexity | Multiple refusals? Inadmissibility? Deportation? Minors? Misrepresentation? | Human only |

### 2.3 Outcomes

| Outcome | Next |
|---|---|
| Ready -- Standard | Book consultation |
| Ready -- Urgent | Book ASAP (within 48h) |
| Ready -- Complex | Senior review before booking |
| Not Ready | Nurture |
| Disqualified | Archive with reason |

### 2.4 Rules

Assessment is NOT eligibility assessment. NOT a sales pitch. NOT a gate to reject.
Default: prospect should be booked unless clear disqualifying signal.
Duration: 3-5 minutes. If longer, route to human.

## 3. Consultation Preparation Playbook

### 3.1 Principle

Unprepared consultation = failed consultation. Every consultation must have a
structured briefing reviewed by the consultant before the meeting.

### 3.2 Briefing Structure

**Header**: Name, contact, date, time, consultant.

**Section A -- Inquiry**: Date, source, days since inquiry.

**Section B -- Readiness**: Program interest, location, urgency, history, budget, flags.

**Section C -- Interactions**: Chronological: first contact, follow-ups, notable quotes.

**Section D -- Prep Notes**: Key questions to address, risk factors, suggested approach.

### 3.3 Delivery

Primary: email 30 min before. Secondary: CRM note. Backup: calendar event notes.

### 3.4 Pre-Meeting Checklist

- Read the briefing
- Know prospect's name and pronunciation
- Know program interest
- Know if in Canada or abroad
- Know refusals or complexity flags
- Know urgency indicators
- Quiet, professional setting
- No scheduling conflicts

## 4. Consultation Structure

### The 5-Phase Model (30-45 min)

**Phase 1: Opening (3 min)**
Show preparation. Confirm understanding. Set agenda.
"I've reviewed your inquiry -- you're interested in [program] and you're in [location]. Correct?"

**Phase 2: Discovery (10-15 min)**
Gather detailed info for assessment. Program-specific questions.
Open-ended first, then narrow. Let them tell their story. Take notes.

**Phase 3: Assessment (5-7 min)**
Summarize. Identify pathways. Be honest about challenges.
Never guarantee outcomes. "Strong case" not "guaranteed approval."

**Phase 4: Offer and Pricing (5-7 min)**
State service scope. State fee clearly. State payment terms.
Do not apologize for price. Silence after stating fee is fine.
If "too expensive": respond with value, not discounts.

**Phase 5: Close (3-5 min)**
Three outcomes:
A) Ready to proceed: retainer + checklist within 1 hour.
B) Needs to think: send summary, set follow-up date.
C) Not proceeding: be gracious, ask for reason.

## 5. Conversion Playbook

### 5.1 Principle

Conversion = removing barriers, not sales tactics. Immigration is not impulse.

### 5.2 Four Barriers

| Barrier | Address With |
|---|---|
| Trust | Credentials, preparation, professionalism, honest assessment |
| Price | Clear pricing, payment plans, ROI framing |
| Timing | Urgency education (deadlines, processing times, policy changes) |
| Effort | Clear scope of what firm handles vs what client provides |

### 5.3 Retainer Process

Within 1 hour of "proceed": retainer (digital signing) + document checklist + payment link.
Day 1: check-in SMS. Day 2: walkthrough. Day 5: urgency context. Day 10: consultant call.
Day 14 unsigned: NURTURE.

Retainer must be digitally signable. Payment digitally collectible.

### 5.4 Urgency Education (Not Pressure)

Only cite factually accurate urgency. Never fabricate deadlines.
CRS fluctuation. Processing times. Policy changes. Permit expiry.

## 6. Follow-Up Playbook

### 6.1 Principles

Every undecided prospect deserves structured follow-up. Follow-up = service, not harassment.
Every touch must provide value. Diminishing returns exist -- respect the line.

### 6.2 Post-Consultation ("Thinking")

Day 0: summary email. Day 2: SMS check-in. Day 5: value-add content. Day 7: call/SMS.
Day 14: gentle close email. Day 30+: nurture.

### 6.3 Post-Assessment (Didn't Book)

Day 0: SMS with link. Day 1: reminder. Day 3: call. Day 5: SMS. Day 10: email.
Day 21: final. Day 30+: nurture.

### 6.4 Nurture Track

Monthly email: immigration tips, IRCC updates, success stories. NOT sales pitch every time.
Quarterly SMS: brief check-in.
On policy change: timely alert.

### 6.5 Consent and Opt-Out

Every message includes opt-out. "STOP" = all follow-up ceases immediately.
Document consent in CRM. CASL applies. Express consent for commercial messages.

## 7. No-Show Recovery

### 7.1 Prevention

Confirmation on booking. Reminder 48h (with YES request). 24h. 2h.
If no 48h reply: operator calls at 24h to confirm.

### 7.2 Recovery

+5 min: SMS. +15 min: call. +2h: SMS + link. +1 day: SMS. +3 days: email. +7 days: final SMS.

Rules: never punitive. Assume good faith. Make rebooking effortless (one click).
Track no-show rates by source -- persistent no-shows = lead quality problem.

## 8. Sales Metrics

### Tier 1 -- Pipeline Health (Daily)

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Speed-to-first-contact | < 5 min AI, < 30 min human |
| Contact rate | > 75% |
| Booking rate | > 55% |
| Show rate | > 80% |
| Consult-to-retained rate | > 35% |
| Overall conversion | > 8% |

### Tier 2 -- Operations (Weekly)

Assessment rate, stage velocity, stuck leads, no-show rate, recovery rate, attempts per contact.

### Tier 3 -- Business Intelligence (Monthly)

Revenue per lead, cost per retained, source ROI, avg engagement value, time to retained,
consultation utilization, operator productivity.

### Metric-Driven Decisions

| Bad Metric | Likely Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Speed > 30 min | Team overwhelmed | AI calling, staffing |
| Contact < 60% | Wrong channels, bad data | Diversify, verify phones |
| Booking < 40% | Friction, no urgency | Improve messaging, reduce friction |
| Show < 70% | Weak reminders, long lead time | Shorten gap, add confirmation |
| Consult-to-retained < 25% | Consultation or pricing issue | Review recordings, readiness criteria, pricing |
| Overall < 5% | Systemic | Full pipeline audit |
